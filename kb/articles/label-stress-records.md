## Overview

Stress Records was co-founded by Dave Seaman and Steve Hillier in the early 1990s, distributed through DMC. It was one of the key progressive house labels of the era — operating with genuine curatorial seriousness and releasing records that sat at the centre of what the scene valued.

## The Label's Position

Stress occupied a specific position in the progressive house landscape. It was not a major-label offshoot like DeConstruction, and it was not as psychedelic or experimental as Platipus. It was the label of a working DJ — Seaman — who knew what records did in rooms because he was playing them. That practical knowledge shaped what the label signed and released.

## Jackpot as the Alternative

Soundsation had an offer from Jackpot Records — the label Blue Amazon were on — before signing to Stress. Jackpot was a credible home for progressive house at the time. Simon Latham chose Stress because he knew James Wiltshire, the engineer there. That connection was not casual — Wiltshire had produced two tracks for Simon's earlier band Something Wild in 1990. By the time the label decision was made, the relationship was four years old and based on having made music together. Wiltshire would later go on to become one half of Freemasons, one of the biggest house production outfits in the UK in the 2000s. In 1990 he was producing in Bournemouth.

That choice placed *Do You Feel It?* on a label whose A&R instinct ran directly to John Digweed — who named it his favourite record of 1996 on Pete Tong's Radio 1 Essential Selection. The relationship that led to the signing was also the relationship that put the record in front of the right people.

## Blue Amazon

Blue Amazon — the progressive house project associated with Jackpot — represents the road not taken for Soundsation. A credible act on a credible label, but a different network from the one Stress connected to.

## DMC Distribution

Stress Records distributed through DMC (Disco Mix Club), one of the major UK dance music distribution operations of the era. DMC gave Stress the infrastructure to move vinyl through the DJ network at scale — the same network through which *Do You Feel It?* reached Sasha, Digweed, and the wider progressive house circuit.
