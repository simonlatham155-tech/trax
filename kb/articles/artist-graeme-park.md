## Who He Is

Graeme Park is a Scottish DJ who relocated to Nottingham and became a resident at the Haçienda in Manchester alongside Mike Pickering. If Sasha defined what progressive house became, Park was pivotal in defining what it came from.

## The Haçienda

Park's Wednesday night Nude residency at the Haçienda, running from 1988, was one of the first spaces in the UK where American house music was given proper weekly presentation to a serious audience. This was before progressive house had a name — before it had become a genre — but the conditions Park created (the seriousness of the music policy, the expectation that a DJ was doing something artistically considered) fed directly into what the progressive scene would be.

His partnership with Mike Pickering at the Haçienda is one of the great DJ partnerships of the era. Pickering played more eclectically; Park was the house purist. Between them they covered the range that the night needed.

## The House Roots

Park's background is unambiguously house — Chicago, Detroit, New York. He never moved as far toward trance as Oakenfold or even Sasha at certain moments. This is what makes him valuable to the story: he represents the house tradition that progressive grew from, and his continued commitment to that tradition is a reminder of what was at stake in the genre split.

## Outside the Metropolitan Bubble

One of the things the Haçienda story shows is that the most important things in UK dance music in the late 1980s and early 1990s were not happening in London. Park coming from Scotland to Nottingham and finding the central event of his career in Manchester tells a different story about the geography of the scene than the capital-centric narrative usually does.

## Later Career

Park continued DJing, producing, and radio broadcasting into the 2020s, maintaining the house roots that defined his career. His longevity is partly a function of not having chased the trance wave when it came.
