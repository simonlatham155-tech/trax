## Who He Is

Nick Warren is a Bristol-based DJ and producer, one of the central figures in the progressive house scene and the one most associated with its deeper, more atmospheric end. Where others moved toward trance as the decade progressed, Warren consistently pulled in the opposite direction.

## The Bristol Connection

Bristol had its own flavour of progressive house — slower, more dubby, more influenced by trip-hop (which was simultaneously emerging from the same city via Massive Attack, Portishead, and Tricky). Warren absorbed that influence without becoming a trip-hop DJ; the result was a progressive sound with an unusual weight to it.

## Way Out West

His production project Way Out West, with Jody Wisternoff, produced some of the most artistically serious records of the late 1990s progressive scene. *Ajare*, *The Fall*, *Domination* — these were records that rewarded close listening in a way that most club tracks didn't try to. Way Out West occupied the same productive border territory as Sasha's productions: progressive in feeling, but with enough melodic sophistication to cross over.

## Global Underground

Warren's Global Underground Buenos Aires (GU012) is one of the most-loved entries in the series — a mix that captures the melancholy, patient quality of his DJing perfectly. His Johannesburg album followed. Both are regarded as high-water marks for the progressive house mix compilation format.

## House of Om

His House of Om nights in various cities extended the philosophy of the music into a club context with serious production values and a consistent aesthetic. This was the progressive model: the night as a total experience, not just a gig.

## Legacy

Warren has consistently maintained the progressive values that defined the early scene — depth, patience, emotional range — while the wider market moved toward more immediate pleasures. He remains active and his productions with Wisternoff continue to reflect an aesthetic formed in Bristol in the early 1990s.
