## Who He Is

John Digweed is from Hastings, East Sussex. He started DJing at local clubs and mobile events before breaking into the national circuit — a trajectory that was typical for DJs of his generation who came from outside the major cities. There was no obvious path in; you built a reputation slowly.

## How He Got There

His early career was at clubs in the south-east of England. The story goes that he sent Sasha a tape, Sasha was impressed, and the partnership that would define a decade followed. This is not quite myth-making — both men confirm the broad shape of it.

The Renaissance residency cemented him. Renaissance Volume 1 is as much Digweed's record as Sasha's.

## The DJ Style

If Sasha was about harmonic intuition and emotional range, Digweed was about **structure**. A Digweed set was engineered from the ground up. He thought architecturally — where each track sat in relation to those around it, how the overall energy of a four-hour set should rise and fall, how to keep a room in a state of sustained tension without releasing it too early.

He played deeper and darker than Sasha as a rule, and his record selections were less obviously melodic. He was the DJ who made you feel you were going somewhere without being quite sure where.

## Twilo

The Saturday night Twilo residency he shared with Sasha ran for several years and became legendary in American electronic music. At Twilo the set could run eight, nine, ten hours. Digweed's contribution to those nights was the architecture — the long, patient build that made the peak, when it came, feel genuinely earned.

## Bedrock Records

He founded Bedrock in 1999, initially as a label for his own compilations, then as a proper imprint releasing singles and EPs. The Bedrock sound — dark, driving, melodically restrained — became the clearest statement of what progressive house valued over trance: depth over euphoria, patience over instant gratification.

## Later Career

Digweed continued releasing Bedrock Records material, hosting the long-running Transitions radio show, and touring globally well into the 2020s. He is the artist from this generation who most consistently maintained the progressive values of the early scene.
