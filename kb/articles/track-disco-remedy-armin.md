## The Record

*Disco Remedy* was a house-style release on SOS Recordings by Pete Lunn and Martyn — the house-leaning side of the label's output, distinct from the progressive house of *Do You Feel It?*.

Armin van Buuren remixed it under the alias **Gimmick** — one of his early project names before he established himself globally as Armin van Buuren.

## Why It Matters

Armin van Buuren would go on to launch A State of Trance in 2001, becoming one of the most globally prominent DJs in trance history. His ASOT show later carried Simon Latham's vocal work (*What Do You See* with DJ Eco, Episode 424; *Here We Are* with Thorisson, Episode 802).

At the time of the Disco Remedy remix, Armin was an emerging Dutch producer working under multiple aliases. SOS Recordings — operating from a bedroom on a clifftop near Christchurch — was in the orbit of someone who would define the genre's global infrastructure before that infrastructure existed.

## The Pattern

This follows the same recurring dynamic in the SOS Recordings / Soundsation story:

- Sasha and Digweed playing *Do You Feel It?* before it was widely released
- Armin (as Gimmick) remixing *Disco Remedy* before A State of Trance
- *New Direction* in 1998 as vocal trance before vocal trance had a platform

The label, run out of a bedroom on the south coast of England, was consistently in contact with artists before the wider world had caught up with them.
