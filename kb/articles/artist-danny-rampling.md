## Who He Is

Danny Rampling is a London DJ, one of the original four who went to Ibiza in the summer of 1987 alongside Paul Oakenfold, Nicky Holloway, and Johnny Walker, and came back having heard something that would change British club culture permanently.

## The Ibiza Summer of 1987

The story of what those four heard in Ibiza — Alfredo's Amnesia sets mixing Chicago house with rock and European synth-pop at a tempo and in an atmosphere that was unlike anything in London — has been told many times. The important thing about it for the progressive house story is that it was the original experience of music as a full-night journey, of a DJ as the architect of a collective emotional experience. Everything that progressive house would later articulate in theory, Alfredo was doing in practice.

## Shoom

Rampling opened Shoom at the Fitness Centre in Southwark, London, in January 1988. It was a small club — 300 people maximum — playing acid house and early house to an audience discovering ecstasy culture for the first time. Shoom was the ground zero of British rave culture.

The smiley face logo. The white clothes. The fog machines. The sense of collective euphoria that had never quite been replicated in UK clubbing before. Shoom created both a sound and a feeling, and both fed directly into what progressive house would refine and intellectualise over the next five years.

## The Bridge Role

Rampling sits at the beginning of the story rather than its middle. He is not a progressive house DJ in the genre-specific sense — his roots are in acid house and his instincts remained those of someone who wanted rooms to feel united rather than taken on a journey in the Sasha/Digweed sense.

But without Shoom there is no Renaissance. Without the acid house summer there is no framework within which progressive house could emerge. Rampling's position is foundational.

## Later

He continued DJing throughout the 1990s and 2000s, moving through the various flavours of house music and maintaining a connection to the Ibiza scene. He was never a progressive house DJ in the strict sense, but the progressive scene never forgot where it came from.
