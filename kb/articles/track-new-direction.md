## Release

- **Label**: SOS Recordings (Soundsation's own imprint)
- **Format**: Vinyl
- **Style**: Melodic / uplifting trance
- **Compilation**: La Bush: Music From The Temple Of House Volume 7

## BBC Radio 1

Judge Jules Show — October 1998 (3rd, 9th and 16th). Occasionally credited on air as "Soundstation" (typo).

## The Shift It Documents

*New Direction* is named appropriately. The move from Stress Records progressive house to SOS Recordings trance reflects the same split happening across the entire UK scene between 1996 and 1999. The title acknowledges this directly.

The record predates large-scale trance platforms — *A State of Trance* wouldn't launch until 2001. It was released into the vinyl-only, DJ-network-driven trance market that existed before streaming, before podcast mixes, before the genre had its own infrastructure.

## Judge Jules and the Trance Pipeline

Judge Jules was one of the key BBC Radio 1 conduits for trance in the late 1990s, parallel to Pete Tong's role for the broader progressive/house world. His show was the radio home for the harder, more euphoric end of the spectrum. Three plays in a single month in October 1998 indicates genuine enthusiasm for the record.

## SOS Recordings

Soundsation's own label, distributed via Amato (1994–1997) and MOS Music (1997–1999). The move to self-releasing on SOS was a response to the constraints of the major-label experience with *Peace & Joy* — more creative control, distribution through established vinyl networks, catalogue on Discogs.

## What It Represents in the Larger Story

*New Direction* is the moment in the Soundsation narrative that mirrors the macro story of the entire knowledge base: the crossing of the line from progressive house to trance. Having lived the progressive house era from the inside — on Stress Records, on Radio 1, on the Renaissance compilation — the shift to trance on *New Direction* is not an observer's account of that transition. It's a participant's.
