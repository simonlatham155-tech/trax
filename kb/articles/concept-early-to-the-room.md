## The Pattern

Across three decades and multiple phases of a career, the same thing keeps happening: Simon Latham arrives at something before the wider world does. Not through strategy or positioning — through instinct and proximity to the music.

## The Instances

**Progressive house in Bournemouth** — Bournemouth was a handbag town. The progressive house sound existed there in pockets: a few local DJs, the occasional big name passing through. Simon and Pete Lunn went every weekend, learning the music they wanted to make from what was and wasn't in the room.

**Soundsation and the DJ network** — *Do You Feel It?* circulated as a rare pressing through the DJ network before any commercial release. Digweed had it before anyone else. It reached Bournemouth's Opera House before most people had heard of it. Simon heard it playing from a kebab shop and asked the man inside how he had it.

**Vocal trance before the genre** — *New Direction* (1998) was a full lead vocal trance record released a year before ATB broke through commercially, two years before the genre had its own broadcast infrastructure. The direction was right; the world caught up later.

**Audio 101: Reykjavik** — Simon created the compilation to encourage young Icelandic musicians to share their music. Kid Mistik (Bjarki) and Johann Stone both had early material on it. Both went on to international careers. The compilation existed to give them somewhere to put work that had nowhere else to go.

**The vocal collaborations** — across the trance releases of the 2000s and 2010s, Simon worked with producers before they were known names on the shows and labels that would later carry them. The collaboration list reads, in retrospect, as a map of artists discovered early.

## What It Is Not

This is not A&R instinct in the commercial sense — identifying talent to sign and develop. It is something closer to being genuinely interested in music that hasn't arrived yet, and being in rooms where that music is being made before the audience exists for it.

The commercial benefits, when they came, were byproducts. *What Do You See* with DJ Eco reached A State of Trance. *Never Far* with Ari Kyle and Audioscape reached In Search of Sunrise 12. But these outcomes followed from the work, not from calculation about where the work would go.

## The Consistency

From the Bournemouth club floors of the early 1990s to the Reykjavik compilation of the 2000s, the instinct is consistent: be in the room before the room fills up. Make the thing before the infrastructure exists for it. Work with people before the world has found them.

This is not a career strategy. It is a way of being interested in music.
